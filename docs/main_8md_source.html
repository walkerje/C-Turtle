5 ### C-Turtle is an attempt to provide Pythons (and thus Logos) Turtle Facility to C++!
6 As an aspiring future Computer Science educator, I have noticed the value of having visible, tangible output beyond lines of text
7 while in the early stages of learning basic, related, concepts. While hesitant to learn Python at first (it definitely was not my first language),
8 I quickly fell in love with the value behind what Turtles have to offer to a learning environment. It lets students explore Computer Science
9 in familiar territory (Cartesian Planes are taught at a young age), while having something to observe which visually walks them through all of their function calls.
10 
11 With this in mind, I noticed many of my peers struggling to grapple the syntax of C++ after having experience with Python. "How could this be?", I thought to myself;
12 "The core concepts are largely the same, what could I do to help them?". I later tried introducing the syntax of C++ to my assignment partners under terms that would
13 be familiar to them-- which quickly became the inspiration for this project. My peers seemed to learn meaningful information quicker when they have something familiar
14 to work with.
15 
16 ### Usage Of & Building This Project
17 
18 This project contains a makefile to build a statically linked library. That said, in a learning environment, I encourage instructors and students to merely
19 copy and paste the source into their projects, to simplify the process.
20 
21 Under VisualC++ (MSVC) and Windows, the required system libraries are automatically linked (kernel32 & gdi32).
22 
23 Under Unix-like environments (Clang, GNU/MSYS/MinGW/Cygwin, LLVM), you must link against pthread and X11. You can install the development libraries for these by running the following commands in a terminal, provided you are operating under a linux distribution and Synaptic is the preferred package manager:
24 
25 ```shell
26 sudo apt-get install libpthread-stubs0-dev
27 sudo apt-get install libx11-dev
28 ```
